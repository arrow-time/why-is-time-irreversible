# what is time irreversible?

Author: Zou,Zhi Kai

https://orcid.org/0009-0000-4279-1064

Time is irreversible because physical processes are nothing but energy redistribution across space. Energy always flows from concentration toward uniformity and dissipation. This homogenizing trend is irreversible, each step increases entropy, giving time its unique direction.

Time is the measure of change in the universe, not a passive, ever-flowing background. If time were merely a background, we would have to ask: how does the background itself change? That is precisely a paradox. Thus, time is not the stage, but the change that takes place on the stage. 

Every state transition of the universe is the advance of time. At bottom, all physical processes can be understood as energy redistribution across space. From stars emitting light to apples falling, from electron transitions to chemical reactions, everything is a reconfiguration of energy in space. 

Thermodynamics reveals a rule with no exception: energy can only transfer from regions of higher concentration to those of lower concentration, following the steepest energy gradient. This transfer drives energy distributions toward increasing uniformity and dispersion. Importantly, this rule admits no counterexample. Even at the quantum level, if you include lasers, magnetic traps, or any control devices as part of a complete closed system, the overall evolution remains dissipative—energy still moves irreversibly toward uniformity. No known phenomenon supports a truly reversible, spontaneous return to a past state in a closed system without external intervention.

To date, no empirical evidence supports the spontaneous reversible evolution of any closed physical system — whether classical, quantum, or cosmological. All observed dynamical processes are thermodynamically irreversible. This universal fact suggests that irreversibility is not an emergent approximation but a primitive feature of physical reality.

The unitarity of quantum mechanics is an idealized approximation; the real world contains no truly reversible processes. To precisely capture each step of this irreversibility and energy homogenization, we introduce multiplicative entropy. Multiplicative entropy is not a statistical coarse estimate of a state, but an exact record of each energy transfer event: it multiplies together the energy levels of all spatial units in the system. Whenever energy flows from a higher-energy unit to a lower-energy one, this product strictly increases.

Thus, multiplicative entropy tells us not only that the system has become more uniform, but also records the exact path of homogenization through every factor of the product. Because the historical path of energy redistribution is encoded in the sequence of entropy values, time acquires an irreversible direction.
